Claude
Your best pick when quality of thought and writing matters most
📝
Long-form writing & editing
Essays, reports, blog posts, white papers. Claude maintains tone, nuance, and coherence over thousands of words better than any rival.
Write a 2,000-word opinion essay arguing that remote work improves long-term employee wellbeing. Maintain a thoughtful, nuanced tone — acknowledge counterarguments fairly before refuting them. Use clear section headers. Flag any uncertain claims explicitly rather than stating them as fact. End with a memorable closing paragraph.
Why this works for Claude
Long-form structure with nuance instructions, explicit uncertainty flagging, and sustained tone are all Claude specialties.
📖
Summarizing large documents
Contracts, research papers, books. With a 200K token context window, Claude can ingest and faithfully summarize massive files in one go.
Here is a 150-page legal contract [paste text]. Summarize it in three sections: (1) key obligations for each party, (2) termination clauses and penalties, (3) any ambiguous language I should flag for a lawyer. Preserve exact clause numbers when referencing specifics.
Why this works for Claude
Claude's 200K context window handles massive documents in one pass, and its structured output following is precise enough to organize findings by clause number reliably.
🎭
Creative fiction & storytelling
Character development, plot structure, world-building. Claude produces the most distinctive and imaginative creative writing of the three.
Write the opening chapter of a literary novel set in 1920s Osaka. The protagonist is a female textile merchant who secretly writes poetry. Use close third-person POV, avoid clichés, and let the setting emerge through sensory detail rather than exposition. Aim for ~900 words with a closing line that creates unease.
Why this works for Claude
Specific stylistic constraints (POV, show-don't-tell, tonal goal) suit Claude's literary sensibility. It's the most likely model to deliver a genuinely distinctive voice rather than a generic narrative.
⚖️
Nuanced analysis & reasoning
Legal memos, policy analysis, ethical dilemmas. Claude excels at holding multiple perspectives and hedging appropriately when uncertain.
Analyze the ethical tradeoffs of mandatory vaccination policy from three frameworks: utilitarian, libertarian, and communitarian. For each, present the strongest case and its most significant counterargument. Conclude with your assessment of where the most defensible middle ground lies, noting where you're uncertain.
Why this works for Claude
Multi-perspective ethical reasoning with explicit uncertainty acknowledgment is Claude's strongest mode — it's trained to hold competing ideas without collapsing into one-sided conclusions.
💻
Developer workflows (API)
Claude Code, agentic tasks, instruction-following pipelines. The API is best-in-class for complex, multi-step developer workflows.
You are an AI agent in a multi-step pipeline. Your task: (1) read the JSON schema provided, (2) generate a TypeScript interface matching it exactly, (3) write a validation function that checks all required fields and returns typed errors, (4) output only valid TypeScript with no prose. Schema: [paste schema]
Why this works for Claude
Claude's instruction-following precision and "output only code" compliance make it ideal for agentic pipelines where unexpected prose or formatting breaks downstream processes.
🧑‍🏫
Teaching & explanation
Breaking down complex concepts with clarity, patience, and accurate calibration. Claude won't fake confidence when it doesn't know something.
Explain how transformer attention mechanisms work to someone who understands Python and basic linear algebra but has never studied ML. Use a concrete analogy first, then build up to the math. If any part of your explanation involves simplifications that could mislead at a deeper level, flag them explicitly.
Why this works for Claude
Asking Claude to flag simplifications leverages its calibrated honesty — it will genuinely note where analogies break down rather than leaving the learner with a subtly wrong mental model.

ChatGPT
Your best pick for technical power, creative tools, and a mature ecosystem
🧮
Advanced math & hard reasoning
Proofs, quantitative problems, logic puzzles. With o1/o3 models, ChatGPT leads on formal reasoning benchmarks by a wide margin.
Prove that there are infinitely many prime numbers using at least two distinct methods. For each proof, identify the core logical mechanism and explain why it is logically airtight. Then describe a known failed approach and where its reasoning breaks down.
Why this works for ChatGPT
o1/o3 models are purpose-built for formal reasoning chains. Asking for multiple proof methods and failure analysis pushes them into their highest-value mode: rigorous, step-by-step logical verification.
🎨
Image generation
DALL·E 3 is deeply integrated and produces high-quality, prompt-faithful images. The only option here if you need visuals without a separate tool.
Generate an image: a lone lighthouse on a rocky coastline at golden hour, painterly style reminiscent of Winslow Homer, dramatic storm clouds breaking in the background, warm amber light hitting the white tower, no text or watermarks, wide landscape format.
Why this works for ChatGPT
DALL·E 3 responds well to detailed compositional instructions and style references. Specificity (lighting, artist reference, format) dramatically improves output quality over vague prompts.
📊
Data analysis & charts
Upload CSVs, run Python, generate visualizations — all inside the chat. Advanced Data Analysis (Code Interpreter) is a killer feature.
I'm uploading a CSV of 500 customer transactions (columns: date, category, amount, region). Analyze the data, identify the top 3 revenue-driving categories per region, flag any anomalies in monthly spend, and produce a bar chart comparing regional totals. Show your Python code step by step.
Why this works for ChatGPT
Code Interpreter executes Python directly on your file, generates charts inline, and shows its work — an end-to-end data workflow no other model matches in a single session.
🎙️
Voice conversations
Real-time, natural spoken dialogue. ChatGPT's Advanced Voice Mode is the most polished voice AI experience available today.
[Spoken] Let's do a mock job interview. You're a senior engineering manager at a mid-size SaaS company. Ask me behavioral questions one at a time, give me 30 seconds to respond, then give brief coaching feedback before moving to the next question. Start now.
Why this works for ChatGPT
Advanced Voice Mode handles turn-taking, natural pacing, and real-time feedback delivery in spoken form — a structured role-play like this is impossible to replicate with text-only models.
🔌
Plugin & tool integrations
The most mature third-party ecosystem. If you need AI connected to a specific SaaS tool, ChatGPT likely has a plugin or GPT for it.
Using the Zapier plugin, create a workflow that: (1) monitors my Gmail for emails with "invoice" in the subject, (2) extracts the sender, amount, and due date, (3) adds a row to my Google Sheet named "Invoice Tracker", (4) sends me a Slack DM summary. Walk me through each step.
Why this works for ChatGPT
ChatGPT's plugin ecosystem lets it orchestrate real actions across third-party tools in a single conversation — this multi-app workflow would require custom code without it.
🌐
Coding assistance
Debugging, code generation, architecture help. Strong across languages, especially with o3 for complex algorithmic challenges.
Here's a Python function that's timing out on inputs over 10,000 elements: [paste code]. Diagnose the performance bottleneck, explain the algorithmic complexity of the current approach, then rewrite it to run in O(n log n) or better. Include before/after complexity analysis and a test case that demonstrates the improvement.
Why this works for ChatGPT
o3's reasoning depth shines on algorithmic optimization — it can trace complexity, identify the bottleneck, and verify the fix is correct rather than just plausible.
Not the best choice for
Budget-conscious users (o1/o3 costly)
Tasks needing humble uncertainty
Ultra-long document ingestion
Real-time Google-integrated search
Gemini
Your best pick when real-time info, scale, or Google integration matters
📰
Current events & live research
Real-time Google Search baked in. Gemini knows what happened today. Essential for news, market updates, sports, and fast-moving topics.
Search the web for the three biggest AI news stories from the past 48 hours. For each: summarize what happened, why it matters, and which companies or researchers are involved. Then give me a one-paragraph "so what" synthesis of what these stories collectively signal about the industry's direction.
Why this works for Gemini
Gemini's native Google Search integration means it can pull genuinely current results — no other model can reliably answer "past 48 hours" without hallucinating or falling back on stale training data.
🎬
Video & multimedia understanding
Analyze YouTube videos, process audio, interpret complex visuals. Gemini's native multimodal architecture is the strongest of the three.
Here is a 20-minute product demo video [upload or link]. Watch the full video and: (1) extract every feature demonstrated with timestamps, (2) identify any bugs or UX friction moments the presenter glosses over, (3) draft a competitive analysis comparing what you see to known alternatives.
Why this works for Gemini
Gemini's native video understanding lets it process the entire video as input — extracting timestamped events and subtle UX issues from a recording is a uniquely multimodal task no text-only model can do.
🌍
Multilingual tasks
Translation, non-English writing, cross-lingual analysis. Built on Google Translate's backbone — the best choice for non-English work.
Translate the following customer support email from English into French, Brazilian Portuguese, and Japanese. Preserve a professional but warm tone in each language, adapt any idioms culturally rather than literally, and flag any concepts that don't translate cleanly. [paste email]
Why this works for Gemini
Gemini's Google Translate backbone gives it best-in-class multilingual output. Asking it to flag non-translatable concepts leverages its cross-lingual awareness rather than just word-for-word substitution.
📂
Massive document processing
With a 1M token context window, Gemini 1.5 Pro can process entire codebases, books, or hours of video transcripts in a single prompt.
Here is our entire backend codebase [paste ~200K tokens of code]. Perform a full audit: (1) identify all database calls that lack error handling, (2) flag any hardcoded credentials or secrets, (3) list functions with cyclomatic complexity above 10, (4) suggest the three highest-priority refactors.
Why this works for Gemini
A 1M token context window means Gemini can ingest an entire large codebase at once — cross-file analysis (like tracing all DB calls) is only possible when the whole codebase fits in context simultaneously.
📱
Android / Google Workspace users
Deeply embedded in Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Android. If you live in Google's ecosystem, Gemini is the most frictionless choice.
Look at the last 10 emails in my Gmail inbox, then open my Google Doc titled "Q2 Planning" and cross-reference any action items mentioned in emails against the tasks already listed in the doc. Add any missing items to the doc under a new section called "Inbox-Derived Actions" with the sender's name and date.
Why this works for Gemini
Gemini's deep integration with Gmail and Google Docs means it can read both sources natively and write back to Docs — a cross-app workflow that requires no copy-pasting or third-party tools.
☁️
Enterprise Google Cloud workloads
Vertex AI integration, GCP tooling, and enterprise compliance. Best for teams already standardized on Google Cloud infrastructure.
We're running a data pipeline on Google Cloud (BigQuery + Cloud Functions + Pub/Sub). It's processing 50M events/day but latency has degraded 40% over the past month. Diagnose the most likely bottlenecks given this architecture, suggest specific GCP configuration changes to investigate first, and outline a load-testing plan to confirm the fix.
Why this works for Gemini
Gemini's deep GCP training means it understands BigQuery quotas, Pub/Sub throughput limits, and Cloud Functions cold start behavior with specificity that generic models can't match.
